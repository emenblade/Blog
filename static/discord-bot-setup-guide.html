Part 1 of 4

Create your bot in Discord

Think of this as registering your bot's identity — giving it a name, a profile picture, and getting the secret key that makes it work.

1

Open the Discord Developer Portal

This is where Discord lets you create bots. You just need to be logged into your Discord account.

Open Developer Portal →
2

Create a New Application

Click the New Application button in the top right corner. Give it whatever name you'd like — this is your bot's name that will show up in Discord.

✨ Pick something fun! Your girls club members will see this name when the bot posts messages.
3

Give it a profile picture

On the General Information page, scroll down to find the App Icon section. Click it to upload an image — a cute logo, your club's icon, whatever you like!

4

Go to the Bot tab

In the left sidebar, click Bot. Then click Add Bot and confirm. This is what turns your application into an actual bot that can join servers.

5

Turn on the required permissions

Scroll down to Privileged Gateway Intents and turn on both of these:

Server Members Intent
Message Content Intent

Then click Save Changes at the bottom.

6

Copy your bot token — keep it secret!

Click the Reset Token button, confirm, and then copy the token that appears. Save it somewhere safe like a notes app — you'll need it soon.

⚠️ Never share this token with anyone or post it publicly. It gives complete control over your bot. If you ever accidentally share it, come back here and click Reset Token again.

Part 2 of 4

Deploy the bot for free

Railway is a free cloud service that will run your bot 24/7 without you needing to leave your computer on.

1

Sign up to Railway

Go to Railway and create a free account with your email or Google account.

Sign up to Railway →
2

Create a New Project

From your Railway dashboard, click New Project in the top right.

3

Deploy a Docker Image

Choose Deploy a Docker Image. In the image name field, enter exactly:

taaku18/dms:stable

Then click Add Variables before deploying — don't click Deploy just yet.

4

Add your variables

In the Variables tab, add these two variables:

Variable Value
TOKEN Paste your bot token here
PREFIX =
💡 TOKEN is the secret key you copied in Part 1, Step 6. Paste it exactly — no spaces around it.
5

Deploy!

Click Deploy Now. Railway will pull the image and launch your bot — this only takes about 30 seconds. You can watch the logs in real time, and when you see something like "Logged in as YourBotName" it's live and ready.

Part 3 of 4

Invite the bot to your server

Your bot is running in the cloud, but it needs an invite link to actually join your Discord server.

1

Go back to the Developer Portal

Open your application in the Discord Developer Portal again.

Open Developer Portal →
2

Go to OAuth2 → URL Generator

In the left sidebar, click OAuth2, then click URL Generator underneath it.

3

Select Scopes

In the Scopes section, check both of these boxes:

bot
applications.commands
4

Select Bot Permissions

A new section called Bot Permissions will appear below. Check all of these:

Manage Roles
Read Messages / View Channels
Send Messages
Send Messages in Threads
Embed Links
Attach Files
Read Message History
Mention Everyone
Use External Emojis
Add Reactions
5

Copy and open your invite link

Scroll to the very bottom of the page — you'll see a Generated URL box. Copy that link and open it in a new browser tab. Select your server from the dropdown and click Authorize.

💡 You need to be an admin of the Discord server to add a bot to it.

Part 4 of 4

Schedule your first message

Your bot is live in your server! Here's how to use it.

1

Type /schedule in any channel

In your Discord server, go to any channel and type /schedule. You should see the bot's commands pop up as suggestions. If nothing appears, wait a minute and try again — sometimes slash commands take a moment to register.

2

Use /schedule create to set up a message

Click /schedule create and a popup form will appear asking for:

💬 Message — what you want the bot to say (can include links, images, text, anything)
🕐 Time — when to send it. You can write things like next Friday at 9am, in 2 hours, December 25, or a specific date and time
🌍 Timezone — type your city or timezone, like America/Edmonton or America/Toronto
🔁 Repeat — how many minutes between repeats. Set 0 for a one-time message, 10080 for weekly, 1440 for daily
3

All the commands at a glance

/schedule create Schedule a new message (one-time or repeating)
/schedule list See all your upcoming scheduled messages
/schedule edit Change the content of a scheduled message
/schedule delete Cancel a scheduled message
🎉

You're all set!

Your bot is live, running for free, and ready to send messages on whatever schedule you choose.
